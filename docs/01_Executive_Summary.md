## 1.1 Product Overview

TriLiska is a **hybrid tax compliance and business management ecosystem** developed by Trident Aliska Digital Tech Ghana LTD under a formal Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA). It serves as the authorised middleware layer connecting MSME business operations — conducted on ERPNext-powered business engines and Flutter-based point-of-sale terminals deployed on Sunmi Android hardware — with GRA tax systems.

TriLiska does not replace GRA tax infrastructure, nor does it constitute a standalone accounting or payment platform. It exists specifically to make GRA's Modified Tax and Business Management Programme operationally viable for Ghana's informal and semi-formal MSME sector, beginning with Phase 1 deployment at **Makola Market** and the **Accra Central Business District**.

### Core Functions

| # | Function | Description |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Access & Identity Control** | Enforces authorised-user-only interaction with GRA systems; identity anchored to Ghana Card and GRA TIN |
| 2 | **MSME Registration & Enrolment** | Onboards MSMEs into the GRA Modified Tax programme with structured data capture and validation |
| 3 | **Tax Report Capture & Submission** | Receives, validates, and submits GRA-approved tax outputs generated by the ERPNext tax engine |
| 4 | **Tax Payment Record Tracking** | Records and reconciles payment evidence against submitted tax obligations; maintains a tamper-evident evidence vault |
| 5 | **Susu & Informal Loan Management** | Tracks daily Susu contributions and micro-loan obligations for enrolled MSMEs via custom ERPNext modules |

## 1.2 Strategic Context & Problem Statement

Ghana's informal economy constitutes a substantial share of national economic activity, yet a significant proportion of MSMEs remain outside the formal tax net — not through deliberate evasion, but due to structural barriers: limited connectivity, unfamiliar compliance processes, unaffordable accounting infrastructure, and low trust in digital government systems. The GRA Modified Tax programme is designed to close this gap. TriLiska is the operational instrument through which that programme reaches traders on the ground.

For GRA to commit programme resources and regulatory credibility to a third-party technology deployment, five confidence conditions must be satisfied:

| GRA Confidence Requirement | TriLiska Response |
|---|---|
| MSMEs can realistically comply with monthly tax reporting | Offline-first Flutter POS on Sunmi hardware ensures compliance workflows function without reliable internet connectivity |
| Tax calculation is standardised and controlled by GRA | All tax computation occurs within the ERPNext GRA tax configuration; TriLiska captures outputs — it does not perform independent tax calculation |
| Third-party platforms can be trusted without GRA losing oversight | The Node.js POS Gateway is the single controlled submission channel from POS devices; all data flows are logged, auditable, and GRA-visible via the ERPNext master |
| Informal and low-connectivity traders are not excluded | Sunmi V3 PLUS and V2 PRO devices with offline sync capability are purpose-deployed for Makola Market and Accra CBD traders |
| Programme-level data supports policy, enforcement, and scaling | The GRA Console and Programme Staff Portal (React/Vue) communicate directly with ERPNext, providing aggregate reporting, enrolment tracking, and audit evidence at programme level |

## 1.4 Ghana Tax Compliance Context

TriLiska operates within the tax regime structure established under the **VAT Act 2013 (Act 870)**. All enrolled MSMEs are classified into one of three tiers based on annual turnover, and the ERPNext tax engine applies the corresponding calculation methodology.

### Tax Regime Classification

| Regime | Annual Turnover Threshold | Tax Treatment |
|--------|---------------------------|---------------|
| `not_registered` | Below GHS 200,000 | 0% — exempt from VAT obligations under current thresholds |
| `flat_rate` | GHS 200,000 – GHS 500,000 | 3% flat rate applied to gross sales; VAT Flat Rate Scheme (VFRS) |
| `standard` | Above GHS 500,000 | VAT 15% + NHIL 2.5% + GETFund 2.5% + COVID Levy 1% |

### Governing Legislative Framework

| Legislation | Relevance to TriLiska |
|---|---|
| VAT Act 2013 (Act 870) | Defines tax regime tiers, VFRS rules, and VAT 3 filing obligations |
| Revenue Administration Act 2016 (Act 915) | Governs GRA's authority to collect, audit, and enforce tax compliance |
| Income Tax Act 2015 (Act 896) | Applicable to MSME income reporting within the Modified Tax programme |
| Electronic Transactions Act 2008 (Act 772) | Establishes legal validity of electronic submissions and digital records |
| Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843) | Governs the handling, storage, and transmission of MSME and taxpayer data |

## 1.5 The Hybrid Architecture — Four-Layer Overview

TriLiska employs a **four-layer hybrid architecture**. Each layer has a defined, non-overlapping responsibility. No single layer performs functions belonging to another.

| Layer | Technology | Role |
|-------|-----------|------|
| **Master Platform (System of Record)** | ERPNext | Central database and master authentication (SSO). Hosts standard modules plus custom TriLiska modules for Susu and Loans. Generates all tax reports and VAT 3 outputs. The ERPNext Website serves as the detailed dashboard for MSME owners. |
| **POS Backend Gateway (BFF)** | Node.js 24 (LTS) / Postgres 16 | Backend-for-Frontend and "shock absorber" between the Flutter POS and ERPNext. Receives typed actions via the Action Queue / Outbox Pattern, queues them with idempotency (`client_ref` UUIDs), and feeds them into ERPNext sequentially. Enforces the Single Active Device Rule (one POS device per MSME). Manages edge JWT sessions and cached tax rate distribution. |
| **Mobile POS** | Flutter 3.41.5 / Dart 3.11.3 on Sunmi Android (V3 PLUS, V2 PRO) | Offline-first point-of-sale and business management terminal for MSME traders. Operates on SQLite via Drift. Calculates taxes locally, generates finalized typed actions (`CREATE_SALE`, `RECORD_EXPENSE`, etc.) with `client_ref` UUIDs, and syncs the action outbox to the Gateway when online. Subject to Single Active Device Rule — one device per MSME at any time. |
| **Web Portal** | React / Vue | Government admin portal (GRA Console), Programme Staff Portal, and MSME tax payment portal. **Bypasses the POS Gateway entirely** and communicates directly with the ERPNext API. |

## 1.6 POS Gateway — Sprint Scope Definition

This document and the current development sprint are **strictly focused on the Node.js POS Backend Gateway**. The Gateway's responsibilities are narrowly defined:

| Gateway Responsibility | Description |
|------------------------|-------------|
| **Edge Authentication** | Issues and validates short-lived JWTs for POS device sessions; delegates master authentication to ERPNext SSO |
| **Offline Sync Queue Management** | Receives chronologically ordered batch arrays of typed actions (Action Queue / Outbox Pattern) from the Flutter POS, queues them with idempotency tracking (`client_ref` UUID v4), and feeds them into ERPNext sequentially |
| **Single Active Device Enforcement** | Maintains the `active_devices` registry and enforces the one-device-per-MSME constraint to prevent split-brain offline conflicts |
| **Idempotency Enforcement** | Guarantees exactly-once processing of each transaction using `client_ref` as a deduplication key |
| **Payload Transformation** | Maps Flutter POS transaction payloads into ERPNext-compatible API structures |
| **Tax Rate Caching** | Maintains a read-only Postgres cache of current tax rates sourced from ERPNext for fast POS distribution |
| **Rate Limiting & Validation** | Protects ERPNext from sync burst overload; validates transaction payloads before forwarding |
| **Error Handling & DLQ** | Manages retry logic, dead letter queues for permanently failed transactions, and operator alerting |

### What the Gateway Does NOT Do

| Excluded Responsibility | Owner |
|------------------------|-------|
| Tax calculation | ERPNext (master tax engine) |
| MSME registration & profile management | ERPNext |
| Susu scheme & loan management | ERPNext (custom modules) |
| GRA Console / Staff Portal serving | React/Vue → ERPNext direct |
| Tax report generation (VAT 3) | ERPNext |
| Payment processing | ERPNext (Phase 2: MoMo integration) |
| User lifecycle management | ERPNext (master SSO) |

## 1.7 Offline Sync Architecture — Action Queue / Outbox Pattern

The offline sync subsystem is one of the most critical architectural components of the TriLiska ecosystem. It governs how business operations performed on the Flutter POS — potentially hours or days after the last network contact — are reliably transmitted to the Gateway and ultimately reconciled into ERPNext as the system of record.

### 1.7.1 The Action Queue / Outbox Pattern

TriLiska employs a strict **Action Queue / Outbox Pattern** for offline sync. The Flutter POS does **not** transmit raw database rows, table diffs, or unstructured event logs. Instead, every business operation is serialised as a **typed, chronological action** and appended to a local outbox queue (SQLite/Drift) for eventual transmission.

Each action in the outbox has the following characteristics:

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| `action_type` | A typed operation identifier. Allowed values: `CREATE_SALE`, `RECORD_EXPENSE`, `SUSU_CONTRIBUTION`, `LOAN_REPAYMENT`, `STOCK_ADJUSTMENT` |
| `client_ref` | UUID v4 — globally unique, generated at the moment of action creation on the POS device. This is the **idempotency key** used end-to-end from POS → Gateway → ERPNext. |
| `recorded_at` | ISO 8601 timestamp capturing the exact moment the action was performed on the device (device local clock). This is the **business timestamp** — not the sync timestamp. |
| `payload` | The complete, self-contained data structure for the action, including all computed values (tax breakdowns, line items, totals). The payload is **finalized at creation time** — no further computation occurs at the Gateway or ERPNext. |
| `synced` | Boolean flag. `false` while in the local outbox; set to `true` only after the Gateway acknowledges receipt with a matching `client_ref`. |

### 1.7.2 Sync Lifecycle

```
1. Trader performs a business operation (e.g., records a sale)
2. Flutter POS calculates taxes locally using cached tax rates
3. POS creates a typed action (e.g., CREATE_SALE) with:
   - client_ref: UUID v4 (generated now)
   - recorded_at: device timestamp (business time)
   - payload: finalized sale record with tax breakdowns
4. Action is appended to the local outbox (SQLite/Drift, synced = false)
5. When connectivity is available, the POS transmits the outbox as a
   chronologically ordered batch array to POST /sync/batch on the Gateway
6. Gateway receives the batch, deduplicates by client_ref, validates
   each action, queues for sequential ERPNext submission
7. Gateway acknowledges each action by client_ref
8. POS marks acknowledged actions as synced = true
```

**The POS is the point of tax computation.** It calculates taxes locally using cached tax rates distributed by the Gateway. The Gateway does not recalculate taxes.

**The Gateway is the point of queue management.** It receives, deduplicates, validates, transforms, and feeds actions into ERPNext sequentially. It does not originate business data.

**ERPNext is the system of record.** Once an action is successfully processed by ERPNext, the ERPNext record is authoritative. The POS local copy and the Gateway queue log are transient.

### 1.7.3 Single Active Device Rule

To prevent **split-brain offline conflicts** — where two devices operating offline for the same MSME generate conflicting, irreconcilable action histories — the architecture enforces a strict **Single Active Device Rule**:

> **ARCHITECTURAL CONSTRAINT:** At any point in time, exactly ONE Sunmi POS device may be the active POS terminal for a given MSME. There is no multi-device concurrent operation for the same MSME.

This constraint is enforced at the Gateway level via the `active_devices` table (see Section 1.8). When a device authenticates for POS use against a specific `msme_id`, the Gateway registers it as the active device. If a second device attempts to authenticate for the same MSME, the Gateway **rejects the login** with a `409 DEVICE_CONFLICT` error. The first device's session must be explicitly released — either by the MSME owner logging out, by a Programme Staff member performing a device transfer via the Web Portal, or by an admin revoking the session — before a new device can become active.

**Why this matters:** Without this rule, two Sunmi devices could independently record sales, expenses, and Susu contributions for the same MSME while offline. When both devices eventually sync, the Gateway would receive two divergent action histories with no deterministic way to merge them — different `client_ref` UUIDs, overlapping `recorded_at` timestamps, potentially conflicting stock adjustments. The Single Active Device Rule eliminates this class of conflict entirely by architectural fiat.

| Scenario | System Behaviour |
|----------|-----------------|
| MSME owner logs into Device A (first time) | Gateway registers Device A as active for `msme_id`; login succeeds |
| MSME owner attempts login on Device B (Device A still active) | Gateway returns `409 DEVICE_CONFLICT`; login blocked |
| MSME owner logs out of Device A, then logs into Device B | Gateway deregisters Device A, registers Device B; login succeeds |
| Device A is lost; admin revokes session | Gateway deregisters Device A; Device B can now authenticate |
| Programme Staff performs device transfer via Web Portal | Gateway deregisters old device, registers new device atomically |

## 1.8 Database Schema Split

Data models are distributed between the Gateway (handling transient sync and edge auth) and ERPNext (the master system of record):

### ERPNext Backend (System of Record)

| Domain | Tables / DocTypes |
|--------|-------------------|
| Identity & Access | `users`, `msme_profiles` |
| Commerce | `products`, `stock_movements`, `sales`, `sale_items`, `payments`, `customers` |
| Tax | `tax_config` |
| Susu & Lending | `susu_schemes`, `susu_members`, `susu_contributions`, `loans`, `loan_schedule`, `loan_repayments` |
| Operations | `expenses`, `audit_log` |

### POS Gateway (Postgres 16 — Transient / Edge)

| Table | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| `refresh_tokens` | Edge session management for POS device JWT lifecycle |
| `active_devices` | Enforces the Single Active Device Rule — maps each `msme_id` to exactly one `device_id` at any time |
| `sync_queue_logs` | Action queue processing state, idempotency tracking via `client_ref`, retry counts, DLQ status |
| `cached_tax_rates` | Read-only replica of ERPNext tax configuration for fast POS distribution |

## 1.10 Success Criteria

TriLiska Phase 1 is considered operationally successful when all of the following conditions are met:

- **GRA Role Clarity:** GRA stakeholders can immediately and unambiguously identify TriLiska's function as a middleware compliance layer — not a tax authority, not an accounting replacement, not a payment processor.
- **Tax Responsibility Boundary:** No confusion exists, in system design or in documentation, regarding where GRA's tax determination authority begins and ends. TriLiska submits; GRA adjudicates.
- **Audit Readiness:** Every tax report submission is accompanied by a complete, traceable evidence record accessible to authorised GRA auditors without requiring MSME involvement.
- **Offline Compliance:** Traders at Makola Market and Accra CBD can complete the full daily compliance workflow — sales capture, report generation, submission queuing — without a live internet connection.
- **Gateway Resilience:** The POS Gateway absorbs end-of-day sync bursts from hundreds of concurrent POS devices without data loss, duplication, or ERPNext overload. Idempotency guarantees (via `client_ref` UUIDs in the Action Queue / Outbox Pattern) are provably enforced.
- **Device Integrity:** The Single Active Device Rule is enforced without exception — no MSME can have two active POS devices simultaneously, eliminating split-brain offline conflicts by architectural design.
- **Governability:** GRA Programme Staff can view enrolment status, submission history, and payment evidence for any enrolled MSME via the Programme Staff Portal (React/Vue → ERPNext) without requiring technical support.
- **Scalability Architecture:** The Phase 1 architecture is validated as capable of supporting Phase 2 national scale targets without fundamental redesign.
