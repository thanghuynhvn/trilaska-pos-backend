## 1.1 Product Overview

TriLiska is a **hybrid tax compliance and business management ecosystem** developed by Trident Aliska Digital Tech Ghana LTD under a formal Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA). It operates as the authorised middleware layer connecting MSME business operations — conducted on Odoo-powered business engines and Flutter-based point-of-sale terminals deployed on Sunmi Android hardware — with GRA tax systems. TriLiska does not replace GRA tax infrastructure, nor does it constitute a standalone accounting or payment platform. It exists specifically to make GRA's Modified Tax and Business Management Programme operationally viable for Ghana's informal and semi-formal MSME sector, beginning with Phase 1 deployment at Makola Market and the Accra Central Business District.

### Core Functions

| # | Function | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Access & Identity Control** | Enforces authorised-user-only interaction with GRA systems; identity anchored to Ghana Card and GRA TIN |
| 2 | **MSME Registration & Enrolment** | Onboards MSMEs into the GRA Modified Tax programme with structured data capture and validation |
| 3 | **Tax Report Capture & Submission** | Receives, validates, and submits GRA-approved tax outputs generated by the Odoo tax engine |
| 4 | **Tax Payment Record Tracking** | Records and reconciles payment evidence against submitted tax obligations; maintains a tamper-evident evidence vault |

## 1.2 Strategic Context & Problem Statement

Ghana's informal economy constitutes a substantial share of national economic activity, yet a significant proportion of MSMEs remain outside the formal tax net — not through deliberate evasion, but due to structural barriers: limited connectivity, unfamiliar compliance processes, unaffordable accounting infrastructure, and low trust in digital government systems. The GRA Modified Tax programme is designed to close this gap. TriLiska is the operational instrument through which that programme reaches traders on the ground.

For GRA to commit programme resources and regulatory credibility to a third-party technology deployment, five confidence conditions must be satisfied:

| GRA Confidence Requirement | TriLiska Response |
|---|---|
| MSMEs can realistically comply with monthly tax reporting | Offline-first Flutter POS on Sunmi hardware ensures compliance workflows function without reliable internet connectivity |
| Tax calculation is standardised and controlled by GRA | All tax computation occurs within the Odoo GRA tax plugin; TriLiska captures outputs — it does not perform independent tax calculation |
| Third-party platforms can be trusted without GRA losing oversight | The NestJS API Gateway is the single controlled submission channel; all data flows are logged, auditable, and GRA-visible |
| Informal and low-connectivity traders are not excluded | Sunmi V3 PLUS and V2 PRO devices with offline sync capability are purpose-deployed for Makola Market and Accra CBD traders |
| Programme-level data supports policy, enforcement, and scaling | The GRA Console and Programme Staff Portal provide aggregate reporting, enrolment tracking, and audit evidence at programme level |

TriLiska is not a legal determination of tax liability. It is a controlled, auditable submission and tracking layer that gives GRA confidence in the integrity of every data point flowing from MSME operations into the national tax record.

## 1.3 Product Vision Statement

> To be the authoritative compliance bridge between Ghana's MSME economy and the Ghana Revenue Authority — making tax participation as accessible to a Makola Market trader as it is to a corporate taxpayer, while giving GRA the oversight, evidence, and control it needs to govern the programme at national scale.

## 1.4 Ghana Tax Compliance Context

TriLiska operates within the tax regime structure established under the VAT Act 2013 (Act 870). All enrolled MSMEs are classified into one of three tiers based on annual turnover, and the Odoo tax engine applies the corresponding calculation methodology.

### Tax Regime Classification

| Regime | Annual Turnover Threshold | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| `not_registered` | Below GHS 200,000 | 0% — exempt from VAT obligations under current thresholds |
| `flat_rate` | GHS 200,000 – GHS 500,000 | 3% flat rate applied to gross sales; VAT Flat Rate Scheme (VFRS) |
| `standard` | Above GHS 500,000 | VAT 15% + NHIL 2.5% + GETFund 2.5% + COVID Levy 1% |

### Governing Legislative Framework

| Legislation | Relevance to TriLiska |
|---|---|
| VAT Act 2013 (Act 870) | Defines tax regime tiers, VFRS rules, and VAT 3 filing obligations |
| Revenue Administration Act 2016 (Act 915) | Governs GRA's authority to collect, audit, and enforce tax compliance |
| Income Tax Act 2015 (Act 896) | Applicable to MSME income reporting within the Modified Tax programme |
| Electronic Transactions Act 2008 (Act 772) | Establishes legal validity of electronic submissions and digital records |
| Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843) | Governs the handling, storage, and transmission of MSME and taxpayer data |

## 1.5 System Architecture Overview

TriLiska is a four-layer hybrid architecture. Each layer has a defined, non-overlapping responsibility. No single layer performs functions belonging to another.

| Layer | Technology | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Business Engine** | Odoo (Community + Enterprise) | Master data repository; hosts the GRA tax plugin; generates all tax reports and VAT 3 PDF outputs |
| **API Gateway** | NestJS | Single controlled gateway routing all client traffic; responsible for authentication, authorisation, and orchestration of submission workflows |
| **Mobile POS** | Flutter 3.x on Sunmi Android (V3 PLUS, V2 PRO) | Offline-first point-of-sale and business management terminal for MSME traders; syncs to gateway when connectivity is available |
| **Web Portal** | React / Vue | GRA Console, Programme Staff Portal, and Admin Dashboard for enrolment management, submission tracking, and audit review |

## 1.7 Success Criteria

TriLiska Phase 1 is considered operationally successful when all of the following conditions are met:

- **GRA Role Clarity:** GRA stakeholders can immediately and unambiguously identify TriLiska's function as a middleware compliance layer — not a tax authority, not an accounting replacement, not a payment processor.
- **Tax Responsibility Boundary:** No confusion exists, in system design or in documentation, regarding where GRA's tax determination authority begins and ends. TriLiska submits; GRA adjudicates.
- **Audit Readiness:** Every tax report submission is accompanied by a complete, traceable evidence record. The evidence vault is accessible to authorised GRA auditors without requiring MSME involvement.
- **Offline Compliance:** Traders at Makola Market and Accra CBD can complete the full daily compliance workflow — sales capture, report generation, submission queuing — without a live internet connection.
- **Governability:** GRA Programme Staff can view enrolment status, submission history, and payment evidence for any enrolled MSME via the Programme Staff Portal without requiring technical support.
- **Scalability Architecture:** The Phase 1 architecture is validated as capable of supporting Phase 2 national scale targets without fundamental redesign.
